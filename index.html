What we're about

DataLand NZ is an Open Data event showcasing the fun side of open government data, and encouraging innovation and connectivity in the public sector.

Our mission: To bring together creative minds and celebrate the fantastic potential of New Zealand Government Open Data.

DataLand is based on a traditional hackathon, with a more holistic twist. In a hackathon, participants spend an allotted amount of time (12, 24 or even 48 straight hours) working on a solution to provided problems, with prizes going to whichever team can provide the most capable solutions. At DataLand, participants will work from 9 to 5 over two days, and work to generate interesting and innovative ideas to address problems provided by sponsors, with prizes up for grabs for interesting, clever or unexpected solutions.

DataLand is planned for Friday the 31st of August and Saturday the 1st of September, from 9 am to 5 pm. We will be releasing team lists and problem statements by Monday 27th of August, to give you time to prepare and brainstorm before getting to work on Friday.

Skills we're looking for

At DataLand we are looking not for technical skills but for a mindset of a love for creativity, a passion for working with people and unique, ambitious ways of approaching solving problems. We are aiming to include as diverse a skillset as possible, in order to help show how powerful a government can be when it embraces diversity and inclusion.

Our aim is to use Open Data and Open Government principles to drive Innovation and Connectivity. We believe that this goal isn't possibile without the help of the next generation of digital workers.
